What is active futurism?

The Afrofuturist praxis has always promoted a revision of black history to build positive black presentism and futurisms.

Abstract

This project addresses the critique of Afrofuturism as naive utopianism by investigating how speculative literature anchors futuristic worlds in historical sites. It creates active futurism by demonstrating its profound engagement with lived experience and historical reality. While scholars such as Eshun (2003) and Zmalin (2019) argue that Afrofuturism serves as a theoretical reflection on Black presentism and freedom, the field often lacks a concrete visual bridge between speculative futures and historical disenfranchisement. To bridge this gap, this project employs an anachronistic framework anchored in counter mapping to create an interactive chronos map from an initial corpus of 10 Afrofuturist literary texts. It will curate themes and spatial descriptors within the selected texts by using Voyant to identify “imaginative geographies”. The anticipated outcome of this project will be an interactive map and dataset of Afrofuturist texts that provide a visual link to historical black diasporic sites and the futuristic re-representations mapped onto these sites. The project aims to be a resource designed for post-secondary educators, students of African Studies, and researchers interested in the spatial history of the Black Diaspora.

This project, at its core, problematizes and defines “active futurism” as a praxis that rejects the treatment of Black bodies as a pastless space devoid of identity and onto which oppressive fears can be ascribed. Instead, it engages with the present and the future by relying on the speculative, rooted in pastness. It is therefore a project that ascribes to the broader Afrofuturist praxis of representing Black futurity through history and highlighting misrepresentations and invisibility (Eshun 2003, Zamalin 2019). We engage with the following questions: How does active futurity manifest in our selected texts? And how does it interact with space and time? To contextualize active futurity in our corpus and move beyond escapism/utopia.
